Executive Summary

Live shopping is a high-stakes, real-time commerce experience where every second of latency costs sales. With 87 test ideas identified across 7 SFDIPOT factors, this epic requires exceptional focus on Reliability (WebSocket resilience, inventory sync), Performance (sub-2s product overlay latency), and Security (preventing bot purchases during flash moments). The FOMO-driven shopping psychology demands that technical quality translates directly to emotional trust.

🚨 Critical Priorities

  • Reliability: WebSocket reconnection within 5s without losing cart state
  • Security: XSS protection in captions, bot prevention for limited items
  • Performance: Product overlay latency under 2 seconds end-to-end

⚡ Quick Wins

  • Usability: Keyboard navigation for all live stream controls
  • Charisma: PiP transition polish for delight factor
  • Compatibility: Safari ITP compliance check (no 3rd-party cookie dependencies)

Reliability
"Will it work well and resist failure in real-time commerce conditions?"

💡 Why This Matters

Live shopping creates a unique "point of no return" scenario: unlike traditional e-commerce where users can refresh and retry, a live event cannot be paused or rewound. If the WebSocket connection drops during a flash sale moment, users lose both the stream context and their purchase opportunity. The SFDIPOT assessment identifies 9 P0 test scenarios, with the majority focused on connection resilience and data integrity under failure conditions.

Business Impact: Fashion Ball events represent About You's highest-conversion shopping moments. A 30-second stream interruption during product reveal could result in abandoned carts worth thousands of euros. With 10,000 concurrent viewers expected for major events, even 5% connection failures would affect 500 potential customers simultaneously.

SFDIPOT: TC-STRU-8A1F3B2C (P0) Direct WebSocket reconnection must complete within 5 seconds 5-second threshold balances user patience against connection stability; longer delays risk users leaving stream entirely
SFDIPOT: TC-FUNC-B2C3D4E5 (P0) Direct 50 concurrent add-to-bag actions must not cause overselling Race condition during inventory depletion is the highest-risk scenario for both revenue (refunds) and trust (disappointed customers)
SFDIPOT: TC-DATA-A1B2C3D4 (P0) Direct Partially processed replays must not appear in archives Incomplete chapter data creates broken user experience; better to delay than show corrupted content
SFDIPOT: TC-DATA-B2C3D4E5 (P0) Direct Product-timestamp mappings must survive database failover Orphaned timestamps pointing to deleted products would cause UI crashes or empty overlays
SFDIPOT: Structure/Service clarifying questions Inferred Caption service failover to Google Speech API within 2 seconds WCAG 1.2.4 compliance requires continuous captions; service failure blocks deaf users from participation

⚠️ Risk Warnings

⚠️ Cart state loss during WebSocket reconnection would force users to re-add items, likely causing abandonment during time-sensitive live events
⚠️ Inventory overselling requires manual order cancellation, customer service contacts, and potential social media backlash during high-visibility events
⚠️ AWS Transcribe outage without fallback would render live content inaccessible to deaf/hard-of-hearing users (legal compliance risk)

📋 Recommended Actions

▼
Engineering
Implement WebSocket reconnection with cart state persistence

Store cart state in localStorage/sessionStorage before WebSocket operations; restore on reconnect. Acceptance: reconnection completes within 5 seconds AND cart contains identical items.

Engineering
Implement distributed lock for inventory reservation

Use Redis SETNX or similar for atomic inventory decrements during add-to-bag. Acceptance: 200 concurrent purchases of last item results in exactly 1 successful transaction.

Testing
Create chaos engineering suite for live shopping

Inject WebSocket failures, database failovers, and caption service outages during simulated events. Acceptance: system degrades gracefully without data loss in all scenarios.

PI Planning
Schedule Fashion Ball dress rehearsal

Run full load test 1 week before major events with 10,000 simulated concurrent viewers. Acceptance: all P0 reliability scenarios pass under production-like conditions.

Security
"How well protected against unauthorized use and malicious exploitation?"

💡 Why This Matters

Live shopping introduces unique attack vectors: real-time caption injection, automated bot purchases during flash sales, and WebSocket protocol exploitation. The combination of FOMO-driven psychology and limited-quantity items creates strong financial incentives for bad actors. The SFDIPOT assessment explicitly calls out XSS protection in captions as a P0 concern.

Business Impact: A single successful XSS injection in live captions could expose thousands of concurrent viewers to malicious scripts. Bot purchases of limited-edition Fashion Ball items could cause social media outrage and damage brand reputation with legitimate customers who missed out.

Performance
"How speedy and responsive under real-time commerce conditions?"

💡 Why This Matters

Live shopping amplifies every millisecond of latency into lost sales. When a host holds up a product and says "click now," viewers expect the overlay to appear instantly. The SFDIPOT assessment identifies end-to-end latency from product display to overlay appearance as a P1 concern with a 2-second threshold. Caption latency must stay within 3 seconds to maintain lip-sync coherence.

Business Impact: Studies show that each 100ms of added latency reduces conversion by 1%. For live shopping where decisions are impulse-driven, even 500ms of "where's the buy button?" hesitation can break the FOMO momentum. With average order values potentially in the hundreds of euros during Fashion Ball events, latency has direct revenue impact.

Cross-Cutting Concerns

These quality aspects span multiple criteria and require coordinated attention across the implementation.

Real-Time Data Consistency

WebSocket state, inventory levels, and caption text must remain synchronized across thousands of concurrent connections. A consistency failure in any component cascades to user experience failures. This concern spans Reliability (data integrity), Scalability (concurrent connections), and Performance (sync latency).

Reliability Scalability Performance

Accessibility Across All Interaction Modes

Live shopping must be accessible to users with visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive disabilities. Captions serve deaf users, keyboard navigation serves motor-impaired users, and clear visual feedback serves users with cognitive disabilities. This is not a single-feature concern but a system-wide design principle.

Usability Capability Charisma

Graceful Degradation Under Failure

When components fail (WebSocket disconnect, caption service outage, CDN issues), the system should degrade gracefully rather than catastrophically. Users should receive clear feedback about degraded states and be able to continue shopping where possible. This requires coordinated error handling across all components.

Reliability Usability Security

FOMO Psychology vs. Trust

Live shopping deliberately creates urgency and scarcity perception. However, if technical failures undermine trust (wrong products added, inventory glitches, lost carts), the psychological contract breaks. The balance between excitement and reliability determines long-term user engagement.
