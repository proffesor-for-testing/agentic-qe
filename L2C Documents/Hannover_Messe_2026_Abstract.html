Title

"Breaking the Iron Triangle: How Agentic Quality Engineering Delivers Value at Speed, Scale, and Volume" Why manufacturing's triple constraint finally has a solution—and it's not faster horses

Abstract (300 words)

When Henry Ford asked customers what they wanted, they said "faster horses." A century later, manufacturing faces a similar trap: organizations are using AI to generate more tests, faster—but more volume without more value is just a faster horse.

The triple constraint has haunted manufacturing for decades: Speed, Cost, or Quality—pick two. Quality Engineering inherited this curse. The 2026 State of Testing Report confirms what practitioners have known for years: 70% of testing capacity is consumed by testing-related activities, while only 30% is devoted to testing that actually creates value. We've industrialized the clerical work of testing while starving its intellectual core.

Now AI promises to solve this—and most organizations are getting it catastrophically wrong. They're using AI to accelerate the 70% (more test cases, faster execution, bigger reports) while the 30% that matters (risk analysis, critical evaluation, informed decisions) remains unchanged. 76.8% have adopted AI in testing, yet only 4.5% measure customer impact. That's not transformation. That's automation of waste at scale.

Agentic Quality Engineering breaks the triple constraint. Not by choosing between speed, cost, and quality—but by fundamentally changing what testing capacity does. AI agents that reason about risk, not just execute scripts. Agents that learn from production failures and encode that wisdom for the next release. Agents that handle the 70% so human expertise can finally focus on the 30% that determines whether your autonomous production line is safe to operate.

This keynote presents a philosophy, a framework, and an architectural approach for manufacturing, supply chain, automotive, and life sciences organizations ready to stop building faster horses—and start creating value at the speed, scale, and volume their digital transformation demands.

The question isn't "Can AI test more?" It's "Can AI help us test what matters?"

The Core Argument: Three Problems, One Solution

Problem 1: The Triple Constraint (Manufacturing's Curse)

  • Speed + Cost = Quality suffers (rushed releases, defect escapes)
  • Speed + Quality = Cost explodes (massive QE teams, delayed ROI)
  • Cost + Quality = Speed dies (thorough but slow, market windows missed)
Manufacturing has accepted this trade-off for decades. Agentic QE challenges it.

Problem 2: The 70/30 Imbalance (Testing's Dirty Secret)

  • 70% of testing capacity spent on testing-RELATED activities:
- Test case documentation

- Environment setup/teardown

- Test data management

- Reporting and metrics

- Regression maintenance

  • 30% on actual VALUE-CREATING testing:
- Risk analysis

- Critical evaluation

- Deep exploration

- Informed decision-making

AI is being used to accelerate the 70%. The 30% remains unchanged.

Problem 3: The Faster Horse Trap (AI's False Promise)

  • 69.6% use AI for test case creation (volume)
  • Only 19.9% use AI for risk identification (value)
  • 56% measured on test coverage
  • 4.5% measured on customer impact
More tests ≠ better quality. Faster horses ≠ automobiles.

Proposed Talk Structure (30-45 min)

1. The Faster Horse Opening (5 min)

  • Open with Ford analogy applied to AI in testing
  • Provocation: "Your AI generated 10x more tests last quarter. Did your defect escape rate drop 10x? No? You built a faster horse."
  • Introduce the thesis: Value at speed, scale, AND volume—not instead of them

2. The Three Problems (10 min)

Problem 1: The Triple Constraint
  • Speed, Cost, Quality—manufacturing's impossible choice
  • Show how QE inherits this: "Fast releases OR thorough testing. Pick one."
  • The cost of accepting this trade-off (defect escapes, recalls, compliance failures)
Problem 2: The 70/30 Imbalance
  • 70% of testing capacity on clerical work (documentation, setup, reporting)
  • 30% on intellectual work (risk analysis, critical evaluation, decisions)
  • "We've industrialized the paperwork and starved the thinking"
Problem 3: The Faster Horse Trap
  • State of Testing data: 69.6% AI for test generation, 19.9% for risk identification
  • "AI is accelerating the 70%. The 30% that matters remains untouched."

3. The Solution: Agentic Quality Engineering (15 min)

Philosophy: Quality is an intellectual discipline, not a clerical function. AI should amplify the intellectual, not just accelerate the clerical. Framework: Three capabilities that break the triple constraint:
  • Reasoning - Agents that understand WHY they're testing, prioritize by risk
  • Learning - Agents that encode institutional knowledge, improve over time
  • Collaboration - Agents that work with humans, other agents, and systems
Architecture: How to structure agent systems for manufacturing contexts:
  • Continuous validation (not gate-based)
  • Production telemetry feedback loops
  • Compliance-aware decision documentation
  • Scalable without linear headcount growth

4. Industry Applications (10 min)

  • Automotive: Validating ADAS against digital twins—speed AND safety
  • Pharma/Life Sciences: Compliance documentation generated BY the reasoning process
  • Supply Chain: Quality assurance that scales with supplier complexity
  • Industrial Equipment: Predictive quality from IoT telemetry

5. The Call to Action (5 min)

  • Stop measuring volume metrics that don't predict outcomes
  • Invest in the 30%: risk analysis, critical evaluation, decision-making
  • Architect for value at scale, not just automation at scale
  • "The organizations that figure this out will ship faster, cheaper, AND better. The rest will keep building faster horses."
